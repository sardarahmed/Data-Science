Q:

Engraçado - cute/funny?

I recently noticed that "engraçado" can mean both "cute" and/or "funny". It strikes me that this could lead to some terrible misunderstandings because, certainly in English, cute and funny are very different concepts. How do Portuguese avoid this potential confusion?

A:

Ele é muito engraçado.
Translation: He is very funny.
Ele é muito bonitinho.
Translation: He is very cute.
BUT: Ele é muito engraçadinho. [the diminutive]
Can be: He is very cute.
And pointed out by stafusa:
gracinha is also cute. [invariable, feminine]
This is for Brazil. But it's close in Portugal too.
Brazil: Houaiss online

Que tem muita graça, que faz rir; divertido, cômico.
Com o propósito de fazer rir: vídeo super engraçado.
Que é espirituoso; alegre, jovial, engraçadinho.

Priberam:

en·gra·ça·do
adjectivo

Gracioso, bonito, agradável.
Chistoso, divertido, espirituoso.

There's quite a bit of overlap there. That said, in most contexts cute and funny are not really the same. Only the diminutive of engraçado can be cute, AFAIK.
